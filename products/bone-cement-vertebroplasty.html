Bone Cement · Vertebroplasty

Vertebroplasty Bone Cement

PMMA-based radiopaque surgical cement specifically formulated for vertebroplasty procedures. Stabilizes fractured, collapsed, or compressed vertebral bodies caused by osteoporosis or pathological conditions such as tumors.

An acrylic-based, radiopaque surgical bone cement containing PMMA (polymethyl methacrylate), formulated specifically for vertebroplasty. Indicated for the consolidation and stabilization of fractured, collapsed, or compressed vertebral bodies caused by osteoporotic or pathological conditions including tumors.

Engineered with a low reaction temperature to minimize exothermic damage to surrounding bone and soft tissue. Its high radiopaque content provides excellent fluoroscopic visibility throughout the injection procedure. Supplied as a two-component system — sterile powder and sterile liquid monomer in adjacent ready-to-use blisters, no external mixing tools required.

Indications

  • Stabilization of osteoporotic vertebral compression fractures
  • Stabilization of pathological vertebral fractures (e.g., tumor-related)
  • Consolidation of collapsed vertebral bodies

Features & specifications

  • PMMA-based, radiopaque Acrylic polymethyl methacrylate formulation with high radiopaque content for clear intraoperative visibility.
  • Vertebroplasty-specific Engineered for spinal vertebroplasty — not intended for joint prosthesis fixation.
  • Low exothermic reaction Minimises heat-related tissue damage to surrounding bone and soft tissue during cure.
  • High radiopacity Excellent fluoroscopic visibility throughout the injection procedure.
  • Optimized injection viscosity Viscosity profile tuned for delivery via syringes or dedicated injection systems.
  • Four pack sizes 12.5 g, 15 g, 20 g and 25 g — covering single- through multi-level procedures.
  • Pre-packaged sterile blister Powder and monomer in adjacent sterile blisters — no external mixing bowl or tools needed.
  • Tested per EN ISO 5833 Mechanical performance verified against the international standard for acrylic resin cements.
  • Sterilisation Manufactured in an ISO 13485-certified clean-room facility — single-use device.
